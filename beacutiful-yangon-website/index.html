Introduction

Yangon (formerly known as Rangoon) is the largest city in Myanmar (formerly Burma). A mix of British colonial architecture, modern high-rises and gilded Buddhist pagodas define its skyline. Its famed Shwedagon Paya, a huge, shimmering pagoda complex, draws thousands of pilgrims annually. The city's other notable religious sites include the Botataung and Sule pagodas, both housing Buddhist relics.

Yangon Bus System

Yangon’s bus system is a real challenge for visitors, due to the large size of the city and the hectic nature of bus travel. There are now an increasing number of air conditioned buses with route numbers in western script. Buses are also slow, but they are cheap – if you want to give it a go, the best thing to do is ask a local.

Myanmar Trishaw

For many years, motorbikes have been banned in Yangon. Small bicycle rental shops are popping up around Yangon and can be found in a handful of downtown hostels. Trishaws (cycle rickshaws) are another way to cover shorter distances in the downtown area, you will rarely be able to get one for less than the price of a taxi.

Myanmar Railway

Just to the north of the downtown grid is Yangon Central railway station, which is the start and end point for the Yangon circular railway. This slow-moving, 46-kilometre commuter loop isn’t a practical way to get around for visitors, but it serves as a fascinating slice of local life. Tickets cost $1.
